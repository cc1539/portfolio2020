Education

I graduated as an Honors College Scholar from the School of Engineering at Rutgers University, with a final cumulative GPA of 3.815 and a degree in Electrical & Computer Engineering. My first three years were mostly par for the course, with a hackathon here and there. Last year was the most exciting for me, in more ways than one; I could focus more on electives such as Quantum Computing and Machine Learning, but as a result of the ongoing pandemic, those and all my other courses moved online.


Technical Skills

Here is a list of topics/skills that I believe are relevant for the positions that I will be applying to in the forseeable future, and some of my thoughts on them.

  • Version Control - As may be evident from how this page and the projects listed below are published, my go-to version control software is Git.
  • Machine Learning - I am capable of implementing classification, regression, and clustering algorithms, and have more than a passing knowledge of deep learning with neural networks.
  • Big Data - I essentially see this as machine learning, but with more emphasis on logistics (should I run this algorithm / store this information on the cloud, or can I lorem ipsum dolor sit amet etc...)
  • Agile Development - I find it strange that a word needed to be made for this; does a developer not essentially do "agile" for themselves already in the process of writing and testing their software?
  • Project Managment - Got plenty of experience with this by working with a seven-member team in my Software Development class.
  • Blockchain - I had a little look into Ethereum a little while back, because I was interested in how it could facilitate distributed computing. Small disclaimer; my skills might be a little outdated, since the relevant languages such as Solidity have been changing at a ridiculously fast pace, even as I was learning them in the past.
  • Knowledge of Frameworks - Spring, .NET, etc.
  • Knowledge of Libraries - I've used lots of libraries in Java, some of the more recent ones being beads, bluecove, and processing if you count that but that isn't too impressive. No, what I want a pat on the back for is figuring out how to properly add libraries for C++, with all the linking options and stuff, and having to re-build the source if your compiler is different from the one the developers used for their binaries, etc.
  • Knowledge of Popular Software - The obvious mention here are Microsoft Office products, which I made extensive use of during my time at Rutgers University. I also made use of Adobe products, such as Photoshop. Coming back to programming, I have used VS2019, Code:Blocks, Eclipse, IntelliJ, and also just gcc without any IDE.
